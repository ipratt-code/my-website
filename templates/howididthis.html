How I did this

This was a complicated project.

Requriements:

  • A computer
  • An email address

Preferences:

  • Backend Web-Dev skills (we will be using Python, Flask and Gunicorn)
  • Frontend Web-Dev skills (HTML, CSS and JS)
  • A repl.it account
  • A Freenom account
  • A Cloudflare account

Part 1

Getting the domain

Go to Freenom. The first thing you should see is a bar that says Find a new FREE domain. Input what you want your domain name to be. For example, ianpratt. Next, you select one of the choices that pop up. Preferrably, the free option. This is why I am assuming you are following my tutorial.


Once you have done this, go to checkout. Select period, and change it for how long you want the registration period to last. Preferrably, choose the longest time that is free for use. Don't worry, you can renew the domain later. Once you have done that, it will ask you to either sign in or make an account, or if you already logged in, it will redirect you to your account and ask you to complete the purchase. Either way, follow Freenom's steps. It might say that it can't confirm that you are a human. If so, wait it out a while and try again. Once you have secured your domain, go to part 2.

Part 2

Setting up Repl.it

Log into repl.it. Create a new repl titled whatever you want, and make it a PYTHON repl. This is important, we will use python to create a WGSI server (via Gunicorn) and a Flask app. There should be a file inside called main.py. Paste this into main.py:

from flask import Flask, render_template
import gunicorn
def app():
 app = Flask('app')
 @app.route('/')
 def home():
  return 'Hello world!'
 return app

If you already know how Flask and Gunicorn work together, do what you like!

Next, create the .replit file. Paste this inside it:

language = "python3"
run = "gunicorn -w 4 --reload -b 0.0.0.0:8080 \"main:app()\""

Again, if you already know how Flask and Gunicorn work together, as well as how to configure ports on repl.it, play around with that. Otherwise, don't change a thing.

Run the repl! You should see a blank page with the words "Hello world!" on it. If you did, congradulations! The most complicated part of this process is over!

Part 3

Connecting Repl.it to your domain

Now, you need to sign up or log into Cloudflare. Click on the thing that says add site. It should show a window saying "Enter your site (example.com)". Enter your domain you bought. (Ex. ianpratt.tk) Follow the steps Cloudflare gives you. When it says to add the nameservers Cloudflare gives you, go to Freenom and log in. Go to your domains, and click on Manage under your domain you just bought. Go to DNS, and under DNS click on Nameservers. Click on custom nameservers, and paste in the nameservers Cloudflare gave you. Click on next, and it should check that the nameservers are there. Once this is done, it will provide and verify a free SSL/TLS certificate. This can take up to a day to finish the process, although it usually doesn't take that long. Meanwhile, click on the little pencil icon next to the window pane in your repl. Enter your domain, and it should reply back with a long, randomly generated link. Copy that and click next.

In Cloudflare, Click on DNS, and under that click on add new record. Change the record from "A" to "CNAME". Then, paste your link under the box that says "target", and under "name" put in "@". Click on save, and now it's a waiting game. If everything worked out well, a while later you should be able to go to your site in your browser, and see "Hello world!" on your screen.
